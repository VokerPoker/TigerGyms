Benefits of Proper Nutrition

Ensuring the body receives enough energy for strength training, intense functional training, crossfit, running, or other sports activities.

Getting the necessary amount of proteins, fats, carbohydrates, vitamins, and minerals from food for recovery after intensive workouts.

Helping to achieve the set goal. For example, the calorie intake of an athlete who wants to lose weight should be on average 30% less than that of those working on gaining muscle mass. Nutrition for intensive endurance training will differ from the diet of an athlete working on improving strength indicators, at least in terms of the ratio of proteins and carbohydrates.

Breakfast

Breakfast should be within an hour of waking up, as prolonged fasting can harm the gastrointestinal tract. On training and rest days, a healthy diet should include carbohydrates, fiber, proteins, and healthy fats.

Lunch

This meal should include the most calorie-dense foods with fats and proteins. When planning your daily diet, follow the plate rule: fresh vegetables should occupy half the plate, meat or fish a quarter, and the garnish the remaining quarter.

Dinner

Dinner should be lower in calories—about 15–20% of the daily norm.

Snacks

The need to add snacks to an athlete's diet depends on the training goal. For example, if you want to increase muscle mass, snacks will be approximately the same size as dinner.

Proteins

Can be added to the diet if there is a lack of protein intake.

Gainers

This sports nutrition based on proteins and carbohydrates can be used for gaining mass, replenishing energy, and slowing down catabolic processes after high-intensity workouts. Before buying a gainer, it is recommended to consult with a trainer, as some contain a lot of fast carbohydrates that are likely already in the diet.

Creatine

Necessary for increasing the efficiency of workouts and reducing catabolism after training. Not recommended during cutting if there is a negative reaction such as swelling.

Fat Burners

Increase metabolism, reduce hunger. In fact, fat burners do not burn fat but only accelerate or block fat absorption, so without training and diet compliance, they will not give the expected effect. In addition, they can cause high blood pressure, nausea, drowsiness, irritability, and gastrointestinal problems.

L-Carnitine

Increases the rate of fat burning, helps to recover faster after workouts, and promotes endurance growth.

Conclusion

Nutrition during training should not only be healthy and balanced but also tailored specifically for you. Many factors are considered: gender, weight, training goals, well-being, percentage of fat and muscle tissue, daily routine.
